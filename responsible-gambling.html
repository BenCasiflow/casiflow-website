Best Practices

How to gamble responsibly.

Responsible gambling isn't just about gambling less — it's about gambling smarter and within your personal limits. A few simple habits can make all the difference:

  • Set a budget before you start each gambling session and stick to it
  • Only gamble with money you can afford to lose
  • Set a time limit for each session
  • Never gamble when stressed, upset or under the influence
  • Take regular breaks
  • Set monthly deposit and net loss limits that count across all casinos you play on
  • Use Casiflow's Goals feature to set goals for each session — write them down, track your progress and keep yourself accountable
Warning Signs

Know the signs.

Gambling becomes a problem when it stops being fun and starts affecting other areas of your life. Some signs to watch out for:

  • Spending more than you planned or can afford
  • Chasing losses to try to win back money
  • Gambling to escape stress, anxiety or problems
  • Lying to others about how much you gamble
  • Neglecting work, relationships or responsibilities because of gambling
  • Feeling anxious or irritable when you're not gambling
  • Borrowing money to gamble

If any of these feel familiar, it may be time to take a step back and seek support.

Seek Help

You don't have to figure it out alone.

If you feel gambling is becoming a problem, here are some steps you can take:

  • Talk to someone you trust — a friend, family member or colleague
  • Contact your bank about setting up gambling transaction blocks
  • Use the self-exclusion tools available in your casino accounts
  • Exclude yourself from online gambling in the national self-exclusion registry
  • Visit your casino's responsible gambling section and set stricter limits
  • Search for gambling support services in your country — most countries have free, confidential helplines

Taking the first step is the hardest part. But help is available and recovery is possible.
